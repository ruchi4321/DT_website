Explore the Culturally Rich Venice

Start your trip in Venice, a city known for its winding canals, jaw-dropping architecture and beautiful bridges. Visit the Piazza San Marco (St. Mark’s Square), the largest square in the city. Known for its architectural grace, this is the heart of the city with its quaint coffee shops, fashionable shops and wandering tourists. Round the corner is the St. Mark’s Basilica, Venice’s most well-known church.Next on your list should be the Ponte di Rialto and San Polo. Once the only bridge across the Grand Canal. But no trip to Venice can be complete without taking a Gondola ride along its winding canal network and enjoying the scenic beauty of the city without having to tire yourself!

The Best of Nortern Italy

Fashion Capital of Europe Milan

Take a walk along the large Piazza del Duomo, the main square and the commercial centre of the city! Visit the adjoining Milan Cathedral, which is the largest in Italy and the fifth largest in the world! Next you can visit the iconic Duomo Cathedral, which is the biggest in Italy! You can continue on to the Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II, one of the oldest enclosed shopping malls in the world. MonteNapoleone, Europe’s most boutique fashion district! Check out the exquisite Italian and international design, jewellery, fur and antique shops and soak in the elegance and the luxury of the place. You can also walk down to the Corso Buenos Aires, another popular destination for high street shopping.

Enjoy the Serenity of Lake Como

From Milan, take a day trip to Lake Como which offers some of the most romantic and picturesque sceneries in the world! Enjoy the beauty of the twin cities of Como and Lecco, spend your time wandering around the beautiful Bellagio, or explore some of the more exciting hikes. For nature lovers, this paradise with an abundance of botanical gardens is the perfect retreat, specifically Menaggio. For those who enjoy the magnificence of historical residences, there are beautiful villas which host classical music events and guided tours in the summer to introduce the history of the place. For thrill seekers, there are numerous opportunities for water sports on the lake such as water skiing, windsurfing, paddle boarding, kayaking, kite-surfing or wakeboarding. And there are opportunities galore for shopping throughout!

Explore Renaissance Era at Florence

Start your day at the Piazza del Duomo, the hub of Florence and home to the city's most iconic landmarks – the Cathedral of Santa Maria del Fiore, Giotto's Campanile, and the Baptistery of San Giovanni which together are a World Heritage Site. The Cathedral of Santa Maria, or the Duomo, is an architectural masterpiece and is the third largest in the world. Continue on to the Ponte Vecchio, a closed-spandrel segmental arch bridge over the Arno river. For those who enjoy history and art, the next stop is Galleria degli Uffizi which houses the world’s greatest collection of Italian Renaissance art. If you want to savour the local food or buy souvenirs, head to the San Lorenzo market. The place has an indoor market, the Mercato Centrale or Central Market, with eateries offering a Tuscan meal, and an outer section where you can buy leather, clothes or souvenirs.

Cinque Terre string of seaside villages

Overlooking the Italian Riviera coastline, these towns are known for their colourful houses, scenic vineyards, seafood and cliff-side hike trails offering spectacular views of the Italian coastline! The most popular hiking trail connecting the five villages is the “Sentiero Azzurro” which takes you along the coast with stunning views of the sea. You can enjoy the local delicacies in Manarola’s Nessun Dorma and can even take up a Pesto making class here. This place is also famous for its wines. You can also enjoy a boat ride on its clear waters or have a beach-day at the Monterosso Beach in Manarola village.
