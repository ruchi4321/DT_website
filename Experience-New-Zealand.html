Tamaki Maori village at Rotorua

New Zealand’s award-winning cultural attraction, the Tamaki Maori village, about 20 minutes’ drive from Rotorua. Journey back in time and immerse in the ancient traditions of the proud warriors of this region at this recreated village in the 200-year old native Tawa forest. The fascinating stories and interactive activities, the famous Haka and other traditional dances, the exquisite Maori cuisine, all of these will make your experience of the Tamaki Maori village profound and memorable.

The Best of New Zealand

Rejuvenate with a Mud Spa at Rotorua

If you are looking to relax and rejuvenate, then head to Rotorua's geothermal baths. The geothermal mud and sulphur mineral water at the Hell’s Gate mud spa have been known to possess healing properties. Your body will feel revitalised after exfoliating with the unique mud and cleansing with the sulphur water. It is said to ease inflammation, arthritis and rejuvenate the skin. You can also take a walking tour across the geothermal reserve, see erupting waters, an active mud volcano, land coral and a hot waterfall or attempt Māori carving.

Adrenaline Rush at Queenstown

No trip to Queenstown would be complete without paying a visit to the breathtaking fjords and the Milford Sound scenic cruise. New Zealand is also responsible for the birth and spread of the bungee jumping craze in the world. It started as early as 1988 with a jump off the Kawarau Bridge. You can even try skydiving to enjoy a rush of adrenaline and appreciate the beauty and natural appeal of Queenstown. And for those who wish to get an aerial view of the city without the thrill, there is the option of taking a ride in the steepest cable car in the Southern Hemisphere, the Skyline Gondola!

Helicopter Hike with Glacier Walk to the Frank Josef Glacier

Take a scenic helicopter ride over the spectacular Franz Josef Glacier before it melts! The Southern Alps are home to over 3,000 small glaciers. Visit Franz Josef Glacier or the Tasman Glacier, two of the major glaciers in the region. Enjoy the jaw-dropping glacial scenery and explore the ices caves, the narrow crevices and the spectacular ice towers. Engage in the exhilarating sport of ice-climbing, while enjoying the ever-changing icescape of the Franz Josef Glacier!

Explore Waitomo Caves and Hobbiton movie set

A visit to two of New Zealand’s most iconic attractions – the Hobbiton movie set and the Waitomo Glowworm caves, is a must-do for every traveller and explorer! Immerse yourself into the landscape of Middle Earth with a magical journey to the Hobbiton movie set. Learn insights behind making of the movies, travel across some of the famous places like the Hobbit holes and click pictures in front of the famous green door, explore the Shire, and enjoy a Hobbit cider at the Green Dragon Inn. Next, take a boat ride to the Glowworm Grotto, a subterranean cavern lit by thousands of tiny magical glowworms, and marvel at the sparkling galaxy of blue lights. The glow worms are only found in New Zealand and attract tourists to these world-renowned Waitomo caves by radiating their luminescent light.
