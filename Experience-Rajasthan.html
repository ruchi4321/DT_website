You will not only get to witness the architectural marvel of the numerous palaces, forts and temples with their intricate and colourful frescoes and carvings, but also experience stunning natural beauty of the place and the rich wildlife found here, all while tasting the state’s delicious flavours ranging from royal cuisines to finger-licking street food!

Explore the Royal City of Jaipur

Start your journey with ‘The Pink City’ of Jaipur, the capital of Rajasthan. Check out the City Palace which gives you a taste of the lavish lifestyle of the royals Next to the palace is the UNESCO World Heritage site of Jantar Mantar observatory. Move on to Hawa Mahal (Palace of Breeze), known for its distinctive architectural features from its honeycombed façade to its rows of tiny windows Next, take a drive to the renowned Amber Fort.This UNESCO World Heritage Site is nearly seven centuries old.

The Best of Rajasthan

Spot the Rich Wildlife at Ranthambore

If you are a nature lover or a wildlife enthusiast, then start your trip with an early morning safari to the Ranthambore National Park – known for tigers, boars, bears, crocodiles and many other wild animals! If you are lucky, you may be able to spot some tigers at the famous Tiger Reserve! You can also enjoy nature walks through the lush green forest trails or go on bird watching tours to various important hotspots. There are also a number of lakes nearby which further add to the beauty of the place. Of these, Mansarovar Dam and Lake are a must-do. It is also the perfect place to partake in activities like bird watching, hiking, fishing, or nature photography.

Enjoy the Serenity and the Culture of Udaipur

Often referred to as the 'Venice of the East', the city of lakes Udaipur is known for its grand palaces! The most famous among these is the City Palace. The famous Lake Palace, located in the middle of Lake Pichola is another landmark sight of Udaipur. The Take a boat ride on Lake Pichola around sunset to enjoy breath-taking views of the Lake Palace and the City Palace! Check out the Saheliyon Ki Bari in the evening, a garden for women with several attractions like marble elephants, fountains, kiosks and a lotus pool! The city is also known for its profusion of zinc and marble. Do shop for a few souvenirs before you bid adieu to this wonderful city!

Enjoy the Rich Culture of Desert City Jaisalmer

Take a trip through this ‘Golden City’ which is known for its forts and its close proximity to the Thar Desert. Start your trip with a visit to the famous Jaisalmer Fort or the Sonar Quila (Golden Fort). Continue your journey by a visit to the famous Patwon ki Haveli. One of the largest and most elaborately carved havelis of the city, it will take you down a history of its glory days. If you still haven’t had your fill of the majestic palaces, then check out the Mandir Palace! This architectural jewel is known for its intricately carved balconies, screen carvings, canopies and stonework Wrap up your day by witnessing the Light and Sound Show at Gadisar Lake After soaking in the culture and heritage of Rajasthan, you can take a Desert Safari to the Sam sand dunes, a short drive from Jaisalmer.

Beautiful Blue City of Jodhpur

Jodhpur, or the Blue City has forts, palaces, temples and houses in vivid shades of blue. This city is also famous for its rare breed of horses known as Marwari or Malani, and the game of polo! Start your journey at the Mehrangarh Fort, a historical fort known for its history and legends. Next move on to the Umaid Bhawan Palace or the Chittar Palace. This sandstone and marble architectural wonder is a blend of lndo-Saracenic, Classical Revival and Western Art Deco styles. take a safari through the Bishnoi Village and get a glimpse of the traditional tribal life of Marwar. Immerse in the beauty of this desert oasis which has an abundance of deer population together with blackbucks and chinkaras.
