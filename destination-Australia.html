Attracting about six million visitors each year, Australia is known for its unique natural wonders, exotic wildlife, cultural richness, stunning beaches, and a range of adventurous and thrilling activities. The Best of Australia

Beaches and
Adventure Sports

With a coastline that stretches over 23,000 miles, Australia has some of the best beaches in the world! There are more than 11,000 beaches across the country, including big wave surfer havens, white sand beaches, and family-friendly ones. You can soak in the sun or partake in one of the many water sports at any of these stunning beaches such as the Bondi Beach in Sydney and Surfers Paradise in the Gold Coast. There is also the iconic natural wonder, the Great Barrier Reef, which is the largest coral reef system in the world and is the only living organism visible from space! You can try your hand at snorkeling or diving as well as kayaking and wave surfing while in this country. Other activities that you can indulge in and feed your adventurous spirit include rock climbing, hiking and trekking in the Blue Mountains, snow skiing in Victoria or New South Wales, mountain biking in Canberra, hot air ballooning in Brisbane, Canberra or the red Centre, among others.

Bustling
Cities

Australia is also famous for its vibrant cities that are sprinkled between mountains, rivers, and wide-open plains, offering a unique blend of art, culture, and architecture. The country’s oldest and the largest city which is known for its picturesque harbour, Sydney, offers the most cosmopolitan ambiance with its lively nightlife and vibrant culture. Meanwhile Melbourne is the cultural capital with its European influence and its famous food and shopping scene. The capital city of Canberra is another exciting destination known for its architecture and museums. On the other hand, Adelaide is the wine capital of the world and Brisbane is renowned for its sandy beaches.

KeyHighlights

Driving along the Great Ocean Road in Australia is a breathtaking journey that takes you along the stunning coastline of Victoria. It's one of the world's most scenic coastal drives, offering dramatic ocean views, lush rainforests, and iconic landmarks.

Scuba diving in the Great Barrier Reef is an extraordinary experience that allows you to explore one of the world's most renowned and biodiverse underwater ecosystems. Watch in awe as graceful sea turtles glide through the water, and keep your eyes peeled for reef sharks patrolling the depths.

Kuranda is a picturesque village located in the rainforest of Far North Queensland, Australia. It's nestled in the midst of the World Heritage-listed Wet Tropics Rainforest, which is one of the oldest and most diverse rainforests on the planet.

Tours in Australia

Here are our best tours for you to enjoy

Melbourne

Immerse yourself in the tranquility of nature at the Royal Botanical Gardens. Wander through the enchanting arcades and laneways, discovering hidden gems at every turn. For sports enthusiasts, the iconic Melbourne Cricket Ground offers thrilling games and a taste of Aussie sports culture. Lastly, embark on a memorable road trip along the Great Ocean Road.

Sydney

Learn more

Marvel at the architectural masterpiece that is the Sydney Opera House. Scale the Sydney Harbour Bridge for panoramic views. Glide along the waters on a sightseeing cruise. For a bird's-eye view, ascend the Sydney Tower Eye and take in breathtaking cityscapes. Find relaxation at Bondi Beach.

Adelaide

Learn more

In Adelaide, a city that embraces both nature and culture, you can have a truly unique experience. Head to the Lone Pine Koala Sanctuary and cuddle a koala, getting up close and personal with Australia's iconic wildlife. For a leisurely day, unwind with a scenic cruise along the tranquil Brisbane River. To capture the city's beauty from above, venture to the Brisbane Lookout Mount Coot-tha.

Great Ocean Road, Victoria

Learn more

One of the greatest road-trip destinations of the country, this trip will take you through picturesque coastal towns with stunning natural views. Located just two hours from Melbourne, the Great Ocean Road is well-known for the world-famous Twelve Apostles, magnificent natural rock structures that are more than 20 million years old.

Kangaroo Island, South Australia

Learn more

Located just 112 km southwest of Adelaide, Kangaroo Island is known for its wildlife and pristine beaches. A must-visit is the Remarkable Rocks which have been sculpted by the wind and wild seas over millions of years.

Uluru, Northern Territory

Learn more

One of Australia's most iconic symbols is the World Heritage sight of Uluru. Watch the sun rise across this magnificent view amidst the desert and be enthralled by the serenity and beauty of the place!

Gold Coast, Queensland

Learn more

The Gold Coast, located in Queensland, Australia, is known for its beautiful beaches, vibrant nightlife, and numerous attractions like Surfers Paradise Beach where you can swim, sunbathe, and surf, Theme Parks, and SkyPoint Observation Deck.
