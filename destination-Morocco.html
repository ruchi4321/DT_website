Countries covered

Morocco's unique past and vibrant culture make it an enthralling travel destination. Historic cities like Marrakesh, Fez, and Meknes await your exploration. The towns have architectural marvels, including palaces, mosques, and academies. Medinas, or ancient cities, have winding lanes packed with vendors. Bright rugs, exquisite ceramics, and gleaming metals are among the many items available. You may also meet the skilled craftspeople who create them by hand. They will explain the process through which they make their exquisite wares.

Morocco's Volubilis is an excellent destination to learn about history. It has ancient ruins. Explore this fascinating area to envision Roman life. Entering a time machine! Morocco's heritage is fascinating. Lots to see and learn. It's a massive time-traveling adventure. When you visit Morocco, prepare to experience its remarkable legacy and interesting historical places. You'll enjoy discovering fantastic historical tales.

The Best of Morocco

Desert Adventures
and Beautiful Nature

Morocco's natural beauty will captivate you. Nature and explorers adore the country. Explore the snow-capped Atlas Mountains, beautiful valleys, and gushing waterfalls. Hike or ride horses through breathtaking scenery. The Sahara Desert experience is the ultimate. Ride a camel through the dunes and watch the sunset turn the desert golden. Listen to tales and music over a campfire at a typical desert camp under the stars. It's unforgettable.

Markets, Souks,
and Food

Morocco's fascinating souks and marketplaces delight the senses. Explore the colorful booths in the busy lanes. Find spices, lanterns, leather, rugs, and traditional attire. From cumin to cinnamon and saffron, Moroccan scents saturate the air. Enjoy Moroccan cuisine. Taste delicious tagines, couscous, baklava, and msemen. Moroccan mint tea refreshes. Taste this magical country's flavors.

Key Highlights

Discover the breathtaking beauty of Dadès Gorges, where crimson canyons and lush oases meet in the heart of Morocco's High Atlas Mountains.

Immerse yourself in the enchanting blue-hued streets of Chefchaouen, Morocco's 'Blue City,' where vibrant colors and mountain vistas create a one-of-a-kind experience.

Step back in time and witness the ancient art of leather tanning at the Fez Tannery, where vibrant colors and age-old techniques bring Morocco's history to life.

Tours in Morocco

Casablanca

    Discover Casablanca, a city of contrasts, where the majestic Hassan II Mosque meets the lively Corniche, and where the Central Market and Old Medina invite you to savor the city's diverse flavors and rich history.

Fez

Learn more
    Uncover the enchanting secrets of Fez, where the UNESCO-listed Medina reveals ancient wonders like the Bou Inania Madrasa, centuries-old tanneries, and vibrant marketplaces, all steeped in history and culture.

Merzouga

Learn more
    Merzouga beckons with awe-inspiring desert adventures amidst the breathtaking dunes of Erg Chebbi. Embark on camel treks, sleep under the stars in desert tents, and witness the magical sunrise and sunset over this Moroccan wonder.

Ouirgane Valley

Learn more
    Escape to the serene Ouirgane Valley in the Atlas Mountains, a paradise for hikers and nature lovers, where picturesque villages and the stunning Tin Mal Mosque await exploration.

Marrakech

Learn more
Wander the crowded souks of Marrakesh and haggle for one-of-a-kind finds. Take a tour of the Bahia Palace and its beautiful grounds. The Koutoubia Mosque is a must-see and a symbol of Marrakech. Jemaa el-Fnaa Plaza is a hub of activity, with its many food vendors, street performers, and traditional music

Ouarzazate

Learn more
Learn more about Ouarzazate, the "Hollywood of Morocco," and its rich movie history. The majestic kasbah of Ait Ben Haddou, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is well worth a visit. Visit the tranquil Ouirgane Valley and the stunning Atlas Mountains after that.

Ifran

Learn more
The route from Fez to Merzouga includes stops in the picturesque towns of Ifran and Midelt. Visit Erfoud and take in the breathtaking scenery of the Ziz Valley before continuing to the charming oasis village of Merzouga.
