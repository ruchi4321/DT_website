The Best of India

A Tapestry of
Culture and Heritage

India is one of the oldest civilizations. The country's architectural treasures range from Rajasthan's palaces to Varanasi's temples. The Taj Mahal, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, symbolizes everlasting love. India has 1.3 billion people and a diverse culture. Each region's food, language, and culture provide unique experiences. India's cultural legacy will captivate you, from Holi and Diwali to Bharatanatyam and Kathak.

Enchanting
Natural Landscapes

India has stunning mountains, beaches, and forests. Adventurers can ski, raft, and hike in the magnificent Himalayas in the north. Kerala's coconut-lined backwaters and houseboats provide a peaceful respite from the metropolis. India has exotic animals for wildlife lovers. Tigers, elephants, and rhinoceros live in Ranthambore and Jim Corbett's thick woods. Safaris offer lifelong memories of these majestic animals in their natural habitat.

our Destination

Bali is the westernmost of the Lesser Sunda Islands. A mountain range stretches from west to east of Bali - a zone of high volcanic activity. The island is famous for traditional wooden sculpture and painting, as well as batik painting on fabric. Balinese dances are theatrical performances, the general meaning of which is based on the eternal struggle between good and evil.

Tours in India

Here are our best tours for you to enjoy

Delhi

Learn more
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site Red Fort transports you to Mughal times
  • Qutub Minar Humayun Tomb has an Indo-Islamic architectural marvel that symbolizes India's past.
  • Chandni Chowk is a famous market with spices, fabrics, and delicious street cuisine.

Rajasthan

Learn more
  • See Bengal tigers at Ranthambore National Park
  • The charming architecture and hilltop scene of Jaipur's Amber Fort are breathtaking.
  • Explore Udaipur, the "City of Lakes," and the City Palace on Lake Pichola.
  • Explore Jaisalmer's colorful alleyways and the Thar Desert's beautiful dunes
  • Take a glimpse of the Mehrangarh Fort, which has great views of the "Blue City" below.

Kerala

Learn more
  • Kerala's quiet backwaters, rich foliage, and peaceful beaches provide a relaxing getaway. Here's what you shouldn't miss
  • On a traditional houseboat, tour Alleppey's coconut-dotted backwaters.
  • Visit Munnar and Wayanad, which has mist-covered tea estates and waterfalls.
  • Ayurvedic therapies and palm-fringed beaches in Kovalam and Varkala await.
  • Experience Kathakali dancing and Kerala food.

Goa

Learn more
  • Relax on Calangute, Baga, and Anjuna beaches.
  • Play water sports, and enjoy tasty treats from the beachside shacks.
  • Explore Old Goa's medieval churches and cathedrals, including the Basilica of Bom Jesus.
  • Arambol's hippie culture offers yoga retreats, ecstatic dancing, and organic markets.
  • In Divar and Chorao, you may see Goan life and explore mangrove woods.

Agra
