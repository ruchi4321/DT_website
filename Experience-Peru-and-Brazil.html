Soak in the Culture of Rio De Janeiro

This 30-metre tall statue of Christ overlooking the city from the 709-metre summit of Corcovado Mountain has a chapel inside its base. You can take the Corcovado rack railway that goes through the Tijuca National Park, all the way to the statue and capture the beauty of this landmark and the stunning views of the city below! Another must-see landmark is the rock peak of Sugarloaf, 394 metres above the sea. Relax by taking a dip in the sea and enjoy a tanning in the Brazilian sun at the 4km-long sandy Copacabana beach. Known for its Bohemian culture, Lapa is the neighbourhood for all those who want to experience the local nightlife and soak in a little culture as well.

The Best of Peru and Brazil

Admire the Majestic Iguassu Falls

Head to Iguassu, one of the natural wonders of the world! This spectacular waterfall spans 2.5 km with 250 different cascades making it a must-see! It is surrounded by a subtropical rainforest which is home to the region’s wildlife of tapirs, giant anteaters, howler monkeys, jaguars, caymans besides having over 2000 species of vascular plants! You can take a Macuco Jet Boat safari in the Iguassu River and run rapids at Devil’s Throat while driving right under the falls located on the Argentinian side of the border. You can also opt to take a walking tour of the falls on the Brazilian side of the border.

Enjoy the Rich History of Lima

Next stop on your journey should be the modern capital city of Peru, Lima. This sprawling metropolis which is known as the ‘culinary capital of South America. Start your day at one such site, Huaca Pucllana in Miraflores which is believed to have been built in 600 AD. Another such site is the Huaca Huallamarca with another truncated pyramid which was the Ceremonial Centre of pre-Inca times. Move on to a tour of the historical downtown to dive into the colonial grandeur of this part of the city while you check out the cultural attractions such as Plaza de Armas and the grand Presidential Palace, the Monastery of San Francisco, Plaza Mayor and the Cathedral of Lima. Wrap up your Lima experience by taking a trip to Huacachina, or ‘the Oasis’, known for its sand dunes and engage in some of the fun activities like relaxing with a cocktail by its famous man-made lagoon or take a thrilling buggy ride down the dunes and enjoy the adrenaline rush from this roller-coaster type experience!

Archaeological Sites of Cusco

Visit the church of Santo Domingo to understand the riches and architectural genius of the Inca period. Next, visit Sacsayhuaman, the fortified ruins from that era which is said to have had both a religious and military significance to the Inca people. Continue on your journey through Inca ruins of Qenqo, a religious site carved out of a giant stone monolith. If the nerd in you is still not satisfied, then head on to Maras and Moray. Maras salt mines provide a spectacular view when you look out across the hundreds of mining lots. Moray is another archaeological site which is one of a kind and will leave you spell-bound by its beauty and the history and legends surrounding it!

Take the Scenic Vistadome on your way to the Machu Picchu

Take a ride on the PeruRail Vistadome as you journey from Tambo del Inca to Augus Calientes on your way to Machu Picchu. This unique experience where you are surrounded by panoramic windows in each of the train’s cars will make you feel one with nature. Hike to see the sunrise from Machu Picchu. The trail to Machu Picchu will take you through the stunning Andes Mountains, where you can enjoy the natural beauty of the area and learn about its rich history and culture. Visiting the Sun Temple is a highlight of any trip to Machu Picchu. This ancient temple is dedicated to the worship of the sun. Finally, one of the most unique experiences at Machu Picchu is seeing the llamas.
