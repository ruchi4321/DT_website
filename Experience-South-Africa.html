Vineyards in Stellenbosch

Stellenbosch, famous for its picturesque charm, ancient architecture, natural reserves, and of course, its spectacular vineyards, is amongst the first and most prominent wine producing areas in South Africa. With over 200 different wineries & vineyards set along its majestic mountains, Stellenbosch became the first wine region in the country to establish a wine route and organize a winery network for tourists.Many Stellenbosch wineries have curated unique experiences beyond the typical wine tasting that include pairing wine with cupcakes and chocolates, open-air picnics, wine safaris, and much more!

Cape Canopy tours in Hottentots

Looking for an adventurous activity? We got you adrenaline chasers covered! Situated between the Hottentots Holland Mountains, a World Heritage Site by UNESCO, are the Zipline Canopy Tours, a nature lover's dream. Here, adventure enthusiasts can soar through the winds, and fly over mountains and rivers, accompanied by birds and surrounded by the breath taking Cape Fynbos. The Cape Canopy Zipline Tour includes 13 elevated platforms, 11 zipline slides, and best of all, a suspension bridge above an everlasting waterfall. Only a one hour drive from Cape Town, offering a fully guided experience for nature enthusiasts of all age groups ranging from 5 to 75.

Whale watching in Hermanus

Whales are undoubtedly beautiful creatures attracting people worldwide in hopes of a sighting. Hermanus, a stunning seaside resort town is widely known and recognized by WWF (World Wildlife Fund) amongst the best whale watching destinations in the world and is in fact considered the official home of the Southern Right Whale. Every year, between June and December, the Whales arrive in large numbers to give birth or find a mate. Many boat based whale watching tours offer exciting up close encounters with the gentle creatures in their natural habitat. Not only them, but you can also spot Humpback and Bryde Whales, Sharks, African Penguins, Fur Seals, and Dolphins! Itâ€™s a quaint little town with lots of scenery, world class F&B, and various museums and adventure activities

Shark cage diving at Gansbaai

Gansbaai is the capital of the world when it comes to Great White Sharks with an estimated population of over 1500. There is no better time than the present to feel the adrenaline rush with a classic South African experience: Shark Cage Diving! An adventure that introduces you to the underwater world of Gansbaai and nothing can be more exhilarating than coming face to face with a magnificent predator like the Great White Shark. No prior diving experience is required and all necessary equipment is provided. So gear up, grab your wet suits, and head down to the historical town of Gansbaai.

Bungee Jumping from Bloukrans Bridge

Bloukrans Bridge is popularly known as the worldâ€™s tallest commercial natural bungee jump, standing at a height of 216 m (709 ft) above the stunning Bloukrans River Gorge. The 451 m long bridge gives its jumpers a double dose of adrenaline surrounded by authentic South African wilderness. Bloukrans has a 100% safety record and celebrities like Prince Harry, Jack Osbourne, and Bobby Skinstad have previously jumped from the famous Bloukrans Bridge. The activity is available 365 days a year and during all weather conditions. An experience offering incredible photo opportunities with thrilling memories that last a lifetime.
