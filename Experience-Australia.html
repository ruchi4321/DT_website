The Best of Australia

Scuba Diving in
Great Barrier Reef

One of the 7 Natural Wonders of the World is the 2000 km long stretch of the Great Barrier Reef in Queenstown! It’s the largest coral reef system in the world which is even visible from space and is famous for thousands of species of marine life – from sea turtles to mollusks, sea urchins, dolphins and sharks! Go scuba diving and get to explore this sea life from close quarters. There is a very good chance of spotting dolphins and whales. And for adventure enthusiasts, there is also the possibility of observing shark feeding or catching a glimpse of a Manta Ray in the Osprey Reef!

Soar above the Scenic Kuranda Rainforest in a Gondola

For nature and photography enthusiasts, there is nothing better than the famous Kuranda Scenic Railway and Skyrail! Embark on a scenic journey in the famous Kuranda Scenic Railway. On this historical journey, enjoy breathtaking views of the cane fields of Cairns and the tropical rainforest of Kuranda. Kuranda is a small village nestled in the mountains of Queensland on the northeast coast of Australia. It is known for its colourful arts and crafts sold at its heritage markets throughout the week. Other places worth visiting around the village are Birdworld which is Australia’s butterfly sanctuary, as well as Kuranda Koala gardens. Make the return journey from Kuranda to Cairns on the Skyrail Gondola and enjoy the stunning views of the World Heritage protected Rainforest through your cable car.

Watch an Ethereal Sunrise over the Magnificent Uluru & Kata Tjuta

For the nature lovers and the early risers, witnessing the Field of Light and the stunning sunrise over Uluru are a must! Field of Light is an installation of around 50,000 globes of light in this desert region by the internationally acclaimed artist, Bruce Munro. After experiencing this heart-warming spectacle, you can climb up to the sunrise viewing platform (Ayers Rock) and watch a stunning sunrise over Uluru as the sun spreads its golden hue across the desert. From this viewpoint, you can enjoy breathtaking views of both Uluru as well as Kata Tjuta National Park with its 36 mystical domes.

A Drive to Remember Across the
Great Ocean Road

Are you a fan of long drives? Then drive down one of the world’s most celebrated roads with stunning views that will make you want to capture every moment! The drive down the Great Ocean Road from Melbourne will enable you to embark on experiences which include swimming at amazing ocean beaches, exploring ancient rainforests and secluded waterfalls, visiting quaint coastal towns and enjoying local foods and wines. But the experience that you will cherish for a lifetime is to watch the sunset over one of Australia’s most well-known landmarks, the 12 Apostles in Pricetown! These limestone monuments are the jewel in the crown of the Great Ocean Road!
